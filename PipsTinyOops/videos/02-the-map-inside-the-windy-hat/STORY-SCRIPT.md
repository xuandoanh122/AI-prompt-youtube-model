# STORY SCRIPT — THE MAP INSIDE THE WINDY HAT

## S01 · Curious hat — 0:00–0:32

Late-afternoon wind crosses a pale-wood pier. A pale-yellow straw hat sits alone on a bench while Pip walks past carrying nothing. The blue ribbon flickers, then a visible gust lifts the hat into a low hop. Pip stops and watches. A second breeze carries the hat just beyond reach in one smooth low glide toward the center seam. The hat skims safely along the boards. Pip lowers the wings, studies its direction and follows.

**Closing state:** hat moving away along the center board seam; Pip following two body lengths behind; red buoy landmark visible ahead.

## S02 · Wind trail — 0:32–1:04

The hat circles the fixed red buoy post, passes beneath a brass bell arch and glides toward a hanging fishing net. Pip takes the shorter inside line but pauses whenever the wind drops, reading the hat's next move. Each gust is visible first in the ribbon and net fringe. The hat clears the bell arch; Pip passes under it a beat later.

**Closing state:** hat beyond the bell arch approaching the hanging net; Pip beneath the arch; shell-shaped side path still hidden.

## S03 · Net trouble — 1:04–1:36

Pip tries to intercept from the side. One wing lifts the lower net edge, creating an opening, but the gust reverses before Pip can cross. The hat lands softly in the loose net pocket. Pip pulls the pocket closer by its visible rope, then stops. The hat's seafoam lining has flipped outward, revealing three stitched symbols: red buoy, brass bell and white shell.

**Closing state:** hat safely held in net pocket at Pip's eye level, lining outward; Pip completely still, looking at the symbols.

## S04 · Map reveal — 1:36–2:08

Pip compares the first stitched symbol with the red buoy behind, then the second with the brass bell arch. Pip quietly says “A map?” and traces the third white shell symbol with one wingtip. A matching shell mosaic becomes visible low on a side gate that was previously hidden by the net. Pip frees the hat from the pocket, keeping the lining visible, and turns toward the gate.

**Closing state:** Pip holds the hat by its ribbon beside the shell gate; shell symbol and lining symbol share the frame.

## S05 · Shell gate — 2:08–2:40

Pip first pushes the gate center; it does not move. Pip notices the shell mosaic is attached to a low wooden latch. One wing presses the latch down, the hinge releases with a small click and the breeze opens the lightweight gate inward. Pip sets the hat onto a shallow wooden guide rail inside the passage. Gravity and the visible slope carry it slowly toward warm green light. Pip follows.

**Closing state:** gate fully open; hat stopped at the end of the short rail inside the garden threshold; Pip one step behind.

## S06 · Hidden garden — 2:40–3:12

The passage opens into a tiny sheltered garden beneath the pier. A central wooden peg has the same white-shell emblem and stands beside a small seedling. Pip compares peg and map, places the hat onto the peg, then rotates the brim until it shades the seedling. The blue ribbon rests around a small side notch. One last gust reaches the garden, lifts only the front brim and lets it settle; the ribbon keeps the hat secure. Pip performs one dry double blink and holds a small satisfied smile.

**Closing state:** hat fixed on matching peg shading seedling; Pip beside it; no loose motion after the brim settles.
