# STORY SCRIPT — THE DOOR UNDER THE MOSS

## S01 · Amber seam — 0:00–0:40

Late-afternoon light crosses a layered moss garden. Tiko walks along a dry stone edge and notices a thin amber reflection under one moss flap. Tiko crouches, says “What is that?” after becoming still, then lifts the flap with both hands. A small rounded wooden door appears in the hill, with an amber glass strip at its base and a simple rope handle. Tiko kneels at a respectful distance and studies it.

**Closing state:** door fully visible and closed; moss flap folded upward; Tiko kneeling left of handle.

## S02 · Arrow trail — 0:40–1:20

Beside the door, one triangular cream stone points around the hill. Tiko follows a sequence of six fixed stones through fern arches and past a shallow carved groove. The stones curve back rather than leading to another entrance. The final triangle points toward the ground beside the original door. Tiko arrives back at the threshold and notices the groove for the first time.

**Closing state:** Tiko beside closed door; six stones and groove relationship readable; sky beginning to cloud.

## S03 · Stuck door — 1:20–2:00

Tiko grips the rope handle and makes one controlled pull. The door does not move. Tiko releases it before gentle rain begins. Drops travel down the moss and collect in the shallow groove, but water stops at the threshold and forms a small pool. Tiko watches the waterline rise slightly against the bottom of the door, then shifts eyeline toward the groove.

**Closing state:** door closed; shallow safe pool at threshold; Tiko crouched, pointing eyes and tail toward groove.

## S04 · Drainage clue — 2:00–2:40

Tiko compares the triangular stones with tiny water streams flowing in the same direction. Tiko says “Not a path.” and follows the groove downhill. A close view shows the streams stopping at a fallen twig and three broad leaves lodged across a small stone drain inlet. Tiko places both hands on the twig and braces both feet on dry stone.

**Closing state:** blockage fully visible; Tiko holds twig ready to lift; rain remains gentle.

## S05 · Open shelter — 2:40–3:20

Tiko lifts the twig once and places it beside the groove. Water carries the three leaves away from the inlet and drains through visible stone slots. Back at the threshold, the pool falls below the door edge. The released door swings outward slowly through a visible hinge and small stone counterweight. Three tiny glow beetles sheltering beneath one broad leaf emerge and cross the dry threshold into the room.

**Closing state:** door fully open; pool gone; three beetles just inside; Tiko kneeling outside.

## S06 · Glow-beetle home — 3:20–4:00

Tiko leans to look inside. The three beetles settle on three leaf-shaped wall rests and their natural amber light reveals a dry moss-lined refuge. Tiko stops, softly says “A shelter.” and places the moss flap above the doorway as a small rain awning. A wet side flap slips from the hill and lands over Tiko's head like a hood. Tiko lifts its edge, shares one smile with the beetles and holds.

**Closing state:** beetles safe on leaf rests; door open; moss awning above; Tiko under harmless moss hood with face visible.
