## Story lock

Tiko sees a thin amber line beneath the moss on a quiet garden hill and uncovers a small wooden door. A trail of triangular stones appears to point around the hill, but eventually leads back to the stuck doorway. When gentle rain begins, Tiko realizes the stones mark a drainage route rather than a walking route. A fallen twig blocks the groove and water pressure holds the door shut. Tiko removes the twig, the water drains, and the door opens through its visible hinge and counterweight. Three tiny glow beetles leave a wet leaf shelter and enter the warm moss room, revealing the door as their rain refuge.

## Dramatic spine

- **Tiko wants:** understand what lies behind the amber seam and open the small door.
- **Obstacle:** misleading interpretation of arrow stones, a stuck door and a blocked drainage groove.
- **Midpoint reinterpretation:** arrows indicate water flow toward the drain, not a path to walk.
- **Climax choice:** stop pulling the door and clear the physical blockage.
- **Resolution:** the shelter opens and three glow beetles safely enter.
- **Comic button:** a wet moss flap drops onto Tiko like a hood; Tiko lifts it and smiles at the beetles.
