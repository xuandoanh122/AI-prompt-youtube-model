# TIKO COMIC PRODUCTION GUIDE

> Landscape 16:9 · 1920×1080 · 3:30–5:00 · six sequences × 30–45s · 24–32 shots.
> Phase 1 Move & Freeze pilot remains a baseline only. New production follows the comic exploration pipeline.

## Production order

1. Lock one mystery, one trail, one midpoint reinterpretation and one discovery in `VIDEO-PLAN.md`.
2. Write `STORY-SCRIPT.md`; keep total dialogue/narration at 60 words or fewer and let images carry the route.
3. Create `ASSET-MANIFEST.md` with exact IDs for Tiko, hero environment zones, clue, obstacle and discovery object.
4. Create `SEQUENCE-MAP.md` with six sequence start/end states, geography and emotional turn.
5. Write a `STORYBOARD.md` per component before any video prompt. Each shot uses one viewpoint and one observable change.
6. Approve canonical Tiko turnaround, hero environment wide frame and important prop references.
7. Generate opening/closing keyframes and reject spot-count, wristband, tail, scale or geography drift before animation.
8. Compile video prompts from the approved actual frames, using movement to connect state rather than invent new story events.
9. Record the accepted endpoint in `TAKE-LOG.csv` and use it as the next connected shot's opening state.
10. Assemble in `SEQUENCE-MAP.md` order; verify the mystery and discovery read without dialogue.

## Tiko shot rules

- Movement expresses exploration, effort or realization inside the story; it is never an exercise cue.
- Use wide shots to establish trail geography, medium shots for safe traversal and close-ups only for clue/reaction turns.
- One hero environment per episode; reuse strong landmarks so the viewer understands where Tiko is.
- Suspense has no jump scare, threatening monster or frightening sound hit.
- Tiko uses observation and physical action, never magic, to open a path or resolve the mystery.
- Re-anchor identity from canonical reference after no more than three connected shots.
- After three failed retakes, reduce motion or split the shot; never repair continuity by inventing a new prop.
